Right to Repair for Farming Equipment

Right to repair for farmers is critical for two reasons: time and money. When a piece of large farming equiment breaks down, its not as simple as just driving it over to the manufacturer for a repair. Farms are frequently very far from urban areas, making the drive to an equipment manufacturer such as John Deere too time consuming. Even when the manufacturer sends out a repair technitian to the location of the equipment, the time between the diagnosis of the problem and the shipping of the replacement part can mean a farmer is unable to do a time-critial task, such as harvesting a crop. Because of this, some farmers have actually continued to use older equipment with less restrictions on repair.

Additionally, farming equipment manufacturers, while they might sell the peice of equipment itself, they often do not give their customers the rights to use the software imbedded inside the equipment, meaning farmers now lack the ability to diagnose and fix problems with their equipment.
